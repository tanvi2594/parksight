# 🎬 ParkSight — Tight 4-Minute Video Script (backup / short cut)

*Front-loads the three things that win: validation, optimisation, 3D. Open the live app full-screen and
let it wake before recording.*

---

**[0:00] Hook**
*(Command Map on screen.)*

"This is ParkSight — Parking-Induced Congestion Intelligence — our submission for Theme 1. Illegal parking
near markets and metros is one of the most *fixable* causes of congestion, but enforcement today is blind
and reactive. We take the Bengaluru Traffic Police's own 298,000 violation records and turn them into a
live, validated, deployable enforcement plan — running on MapmyIndia's maps. Let me show you the three
things that make it different."

**[0:30] The map + the concentration**
*(Toggle 3D city, let it tilt.)*

"Every hexagon is a 150-metre cell; height and colour are our Congestion-Impact Score — not a raw count,
but volume times how much a violation actually blocks traffic. Look at the skyline — a handful of towers
dominate. That's the whole thesis: about 7% of the city carries 80% of the impact. And these are the real
chokepoints — Cottonpet, City Market — found with zero manual labelling."

**[1:15] Differentiator #1 — optimisation**
*(Priorities & Deploy tab; point to the optimised-vs-naive bar chart.)*

"Here's the operations-research core. We don't just rank the worst spots and send everyone there — we solve
a max-coverage optimisation. This chart proves it: with the *same* number of patrols, our placement covers
about 13 percentage points more of the city's congestion than naive ranking, because ranking wastes
officers on hotspots that overlap. You also get the MapmyIndia road route, drive time, and the ROI."

**[2:10] Differentiator #2 — independent validation**
*(Validation tab; point to the three cards, then the controlled check.)*

"This is the part I'm proudest of. We validate against a *completely separate* dataset — independent
congestion and accident reports the model never trained on. 63% of those independent incidents fall in our
worst-20% parking areas — that's 3.15 times more than random. And we push on our own result: after removing
the effect of raw volume, we honestly show the link is driven by density, so we present severity as a triage
tool, not over-claim it. That honesty is the point."

**[3:00] Differentiator #3 — the forecast that earns its keep**
*(Forecast tab; point to the green panel.)*

"Hotspots are persistent — so we hold ourselves to a true future test and even show the naïve baseline.
Our model still beats it, gives hour-level predictions a baseline can't, attaches 80% confidence bands, and
flags 278 emerging hotspots a 'same-as-last-month' copy would miss. That's a forecast you can actually
schedule shifts on."

**[3:30] Close**
*(Back to Command Map; optionally type a question into Ask ParkSight.)*

"And you can just ask it in plain English — 'deploy Friday 6pm near Koramangala' — fully offline. So:
detect, quantify, forecast, optimise, validate — a complete loop, built on the police's own data and
MapmyIndia's own maps, deployable today. ParkSight turns parking data into traffic relief. Thank you."
